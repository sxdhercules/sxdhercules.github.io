Buying a home

Pre-Approval

  1. Mortgage Company
    • This is the first step in the home buying process where you find a mortgage company and get pre-approved for a mortgage loan to determine your budget.
  2. Real Estate Agent
    • Once you have a pre-approval, you can start working with a real estate agent to help you find potential homes that fit your budget and criteria.
  3. Home Search
    • This is the phase where you search for potential homes online or in-person that meet your requirements and budget, with the assistance of your real estate agent.
  4. Tours
    • After identifying potential homes, you can schedule tours with your real estate agent to view the properties in person and assess their suitability for your needs.

Offer

  1. Making an Offer
    • Once you find the right home, you submit an offer to the seller, outlining the price you're willing to pay and any contingencies, which is then negotiated until an agreement is reached.
  2. Escrow Deposit
    • After the offer is accepted, the buyer typically makes a deposit into an escrow account as a sign of good faith that they intend to go through with the purchase.
  3. Addendum
    • An addendum is a document that modifies or adds to the terms of the purchase agreement, which can be necessary if there are changes or contingencies that need to be addressed during the buying process.

Under Contract

  1. Inspection
    • Inspection is the process of hiring a professional inspector to assess the condition of the home to identify any potential issues or defects.
  2. Appraisal
    • Appraisal is the process of evaluating the value of the property by a licensed appraiser, which helps the lender determine the appropriate loan amount.
  3. Underwriting
    • Underwriting is the process of verifying and evaluating the borrower's financial information, employment, and creditworthiness to ensure that they meet the lender's requirements for the loan.
  4. Insurance
    • Insurance is the process of obtaining homeowner's insurance to protect the property and the borrower's investment in the home.

Closing

  1. Clear-to-Close
    • Clear-to-Close refers to the final approval of the mortgage by the underwriter, which indicates that all conditions and requirements have been met for the loan to be funded.
  2. Title Commitment
    • Title commitment is a document that shows the ownership history of the property, any liens or encumbrances, and any issues that need to be resolved before the property can be transferred to the new owner.
  3. Closing Costs
    • Closing costs refer to the fees and expenses associated with finalizing the home purchase transaction, which include appraisal fees, title search fees, attorney fees, and other charges related to the mortgage loan and property transfer.
